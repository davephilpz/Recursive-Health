1. Just why is sleep that important?

Sleep plays a crucial role in maintaining overall health and well-being throughout your life. Getting enough quality sleep at the right times can help protect your mental health, physical health, quality of life, and safety. Here's why sleep is so important:

  1. Physical Health: Sleep is involved in healing and repair of your heart and blood vessels. Ongoing sleep deficiency is linked to an increased risk of heart disease, kidney disease, high blood pressure, diabetes, and stroke.
  2. Mental Health: Sleep helps support healthy brain function. While you're sleeping, your brain is preparing for the next day. It's forming new pathways to help you learn and remember information. Sleep deficiency alters activity in some parts of the brain, which may cause problems with decision making, problem-solving, controlling emotions and behavior, and coping with change.
  3. Emotional Well-being: Sleep deficiency can make you feel tired and moody, and you may feel down or depressed. It can also affect your ability to interact with others.
  4. Performance and Safety: Getting enough sleep helps you function well throughout the day. People who are sleep deficient are less productive at work and school. They take longer to finish tasks, have a slower reaction time, and make more mistakes. Lack of sleep also contributes to road accidents.
  5. Growth and Development: Deep sleep triggers the body to release the hormone that promotes normal growth in children and teens. This hormone also boosts muscle mass and helps repair cells and tissues in children, teens, and adults.
  6. Immune System: Sleep plays a role in your body’s immune system. Ongoing sleep deficiency can change the way in which your immune system responds. For example, if you're sleep deficient, you may have trouble fighting common infections.

In summary, adequate sleep is a key part of a healthy lifestyle, and can benefit your heart, weight, mind, and more.
