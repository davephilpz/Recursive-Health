Dietary Hormesis

How altering the timing and amount of your diet can have profound merits for your overall health

Article Overview

Explore the benefits of willingly exposing yourself to hot and cold.

1. Types of dietary hormesis

Caloric Restriction:

Consistently reducing calorie intake below daily needs without malnutrition.

Intermittent Fasting:

Involves cycling between periods of eating and fasting. Common methods include daily 16-hour fasts or fasting for 24 hours, twice per week.

Periodic Fasting:

Involves fasting for consecutive days or weeks, often done several times a year.

2. How fasting and time restricted eating work

Metabolic Shift:

  • Ketosis: During fasting, the body shifts from using glucose as its primary energy source to using ketones, which are derived from fat breakdown.
  • Insulin Sensitivity: Improved insulin sensitivity due to lower and more stable blood glucose levels.

Cellular and Molecular Effects:

  • Autophagy: Fasting induces autophagy, a process where cells break down and recycle damaged components, promoting cellular repair and longevity.
  • Reduced Oxidative Stress: Decreased caloric intake reduces the generation of free radicals, lowering oxidative stress on the body.

Hormonal Changes:

  • Increased Growth Hormone: Fasting can increase levels of growth hormone, which aids in fat loss and muscle preservation.
  • Reduced IGF-1: Lower levels of Insulin-like Growth Factor 1 (IGF-1) during fasting are associated with increased lifespan in various species.

4. Medical precautions before start a fast or any type of time restricted eating

  • Medical Supervision: Individuals with existing health conditions or those taking medications should consult with a healthcare provider before starting a fasting regimen.
  • Gradual Introduction: It's advisable to gradually introduce fasting to allow the body to adapt.
  • Nutrient Intake: Ensuring adequate nutrient intake during non-fasting periods is crucial to prevent malnutrition.
  • Hydration: Maintaining hydration during fasting periods is essential.

5. Key takeaways for extreme hot and cold

Dietary restriction or fasting-induced hormesis offers a range of potential health benefits, including weight loss, improved metabolic health, increased lifespan, and reduced risk of various diseases. However, it's important to approach fasting carefully, considering individual health conditions and dietary needs. Proper guidance and a balanced approach to nutrition during non-fasting periods are essential for the safe and effective practice of this form of hormesis.

Key points to always keep in mind for thermal hormesis

  1. Plan: Create a plan for fasting and eating windows. Going in with no plan is fine, but fasting give different benefits based on duration and type and planning ahead what you want will be beneficial.
  2. Listen to your body: If you feel too stressed out, chances are you probably are. Stop and do at a less intense level. Your body will adapt and slowly get stronger.
  3. Variety: You body will adapt to different types of fasting and restricted diets and therefore the amount of shock can possibly decrease over time. Switch up time of day, duration, type of exposure etc. to keep your body guessing.
  4. Nutrition: Make sure that you get ample nutrition and fuel (preferably fats to boost entry into fast) during your eating windows. Also be sure to get proper electrolytes before, during and after your fast as the body requires a constant supply for peak performance.
  5. Willpower: Don't give in, but if you must, there are a few ways to get rid of cravings to eat. The drinks, however, will break dry fasts and water only fasts so be careful.
    1. Put a little bit of salt under your tongue. Many cravings may be for salt or other electrolytes and not carbs or food.
    2. Drink green tea, black tea, coffee or other herbal teas (not tea plant). This is obvious, but avoid all additives like milk, cream, sugar any fruit etc. in tea. Green tea and certain herbal teas provide the best suppression as they contain the most polyphenol content.
